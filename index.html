About my life

Welcome to my site! I am currently a senior at the University of Wisconsin-Madison, majoring in Geography, Cartography, and International Studies with an emphasis on Global Health.

Although I am graduating in May, I will be entering a year-long program for my M.S. in Secondary Education at UW-Madison with the intent of pursuing a career as an international geography teacher. While teaching, I also hope to pursue my passions as a traveler, writer, and active community member.

What does that look like? Well, my hope is that in perusing this site you will be introduced to my activities and involvement around the globe. Feel free to look around the following introductory pages- if you still are not satisfied, please feel free to contact me!

Teaching!

Teaching has been a large part of my life, and continues to be as I pursue a career in education.

A few of the valuable teaching experiences I have had includes teaching Spanish at El Lago del Bosque (an immersion camp for youth), facilitating teambuilding workshops for student groups, water safety instruction for the City of Madison, and teaching English as a Second Language.

The picture to the right was taken this last summer in Uganda, where I spent the majority of the summer. During my time in Uganda, I decided to take my educational experiences to the next level and pursue teaching as a career.

As I continue to head toward teaching, my goal is to teach social studies, earth science, and computer technology skills around the globe, particularly focusing on diverse student groups and educational accessibility in the digital age.

Maps:

You can find a few links to samples below that present the various maps I have made, accompanied with a short description of their purpose and significance.

This map intends to draw attention to the literacy disparities among African nations, as well as compared with the world. As the world's second largest continent by population, Africa's focus on education will prove to be a crucial component of its future success.

This map was inspired from a wikipedia page that lacked a map to illustrate where the busiest ports in the world are. It is a symbol-size map that particularly points out the busy port economy of East Asia.

Who cares about refugees' stories? Well, this map is intended to persuade the viewer that everyone should. As a qualitative flow-map, the arrows illustrate the nations to which most refugees have fled while also telling the stories of refugees. The narrative lines on this map were based off of personal accounts of Somali refugees.
